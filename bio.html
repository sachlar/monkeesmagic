The Monkees

The Monkees were made up of four band members: Micky Dolenz, Davy Jones, Michael Nesmith and Peter Tork. Spawned out of the American Sitcom "The Monkees" in 1965. Setting the format for boy bands of the future and inspiring multiple genres of music, the aforementioned boyband format as well as Pun Rock and Rock bands.

The Early Years

During the early years the Monkees manage to create four number one albums, three number one singles and ten top 20 hits. In total eleven studio albums were recorded, a phenomenal amount of music on top of the two Emmy’s they won for the TV show, recording 58 episodes over a two year period. In addition to the popular TV show, The Monkees recorded the feature length film “Head” as well as a number of Network TV specials.

Peter was first to step away from the band in 1969, followed by Mike in 1970. Micky and Davy calling it a day in 1971.

The First Reunion

The band got back together in 1986 as a three peice, Micky, Davy and Peter collaborating again after 15 years apart. More recording of music and the re-lease of old music combined with a successful tour saw the band catapulted back to fame on the same scale as they achieved in the mid-late 1960's and early 70's.
